I use AI heavily. I also throw away a lot of AI code. Here's the workflow that survived a year of side projects.

## The rule: AI drafts, I decide

I never merge code I can't explain line by line. That single rule keeps AI useful without letting it own the codebase.

## Where AI actually saves time

- **Boilerplate** — forms, zod schemas, table components. Anything I've written before.
- **Rubber-ducking** — pasting an error and asking "what would you check first?" beats staring at it.
- **Refactors with a clear target** — "convert this class component to hooks" is a win. "Make this better" is not.

## Where it wastes time

- Anything security-sensitive. I hand-write auth and crypto.
- New APIs it hasn't seen. It will hallucinate confidently.
- Architecture. AI is great at local optimization and bad at "should this even exist."

## My stack

- **Claude** for long-context reasoning and code review
- **ChatGPT** for quick lookups and copy
- **Cursor / Windsurf** as the editor layer

The combined cost is less than a night out and probably saves me a full day a week. Worth it — as long as I keep writing the hard parts myself.
