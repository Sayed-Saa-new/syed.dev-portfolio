Vercel is amazing until you get a $400 bandwidth bill for a personal site. Here's how I keep that from happening.

## Cache aggressively at the edge

`Cache-Control: public, s-maxage=3600, stale-while-revalidate=86400` on anything that isn't per-user. Vercel's edge network eats the traffic and your function invocations stay flat.

## Image optimization has a quota — respect it

Use `<Image>` for above-the-fold hero images. Use plain `<img>` for a giant blog post gallery. The optimization pipeline is metered and it adds up.

## Set spending caps in the dashboard

Every project. Every environment. Do this before you deploy, not after.

## Watch the function region

Serving from `iad1` when your users and your database are in Frankfurt is a slow, expensive round trip. Match your function region to your database.

## Preview builds are real builds

If a preview build hits your production Supabase and blows the row cap, it's still your bill. Point previews at a staging project.

Vercel is generous by default. Spending five minutes on these settings keeps it that way.
