I tried to build three products in one month last year. I finished none of them and got sick for a week. This is what I've changed since.

## Sleep is compute

Every hour under seven costs me at least two the next day in stupid bugs. That's not discipline; that's math.

## One project at a time

Context switching between codebases is exhausting in a way that doesn't feel exhausting until you crash. I now finish or explicitly pause one project before opening the next.

## The weekend has to be actually off

Not "I'll just fix this one thing." Whole days without the editor. My best commits on Monday come from Sundays where I didn't touch the laptop.

## Physical space matters

I stopped coding in bed. Bed is for sleep, desk is for work, phone is for neither during deep focus. Boring, effective.

## Ship less, ship better

The Aegis v1 release took twice as long as I first estimated. It was still faster than the burnt-out version would have been, because that version wouldn't have shipped at all.

Rest earlier than you think you need to. You'll ship more, not less.
