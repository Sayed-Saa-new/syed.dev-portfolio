I'm 17, in HSC, and I've been writing code for money and for love out of Dhaka for the last two years. Here's what the local scene actually looks like from where I sit.

## The community is bigger than it looks

Facebook groups, small Discords, a lot of quiet DMs. Public conferences are rare, but the network is real. Ask a question in the right place, someone helpful answers within an hour.

## Job listings lag reality

Most companies list "5 years React experience" for a role that's really "can you ship a form and a table." My repo [tech-companies-in-bangladesh](https://github.com/abushaidislam/tech-companies-in-bangladesh) started because I got tired of hunting the same list twice.

## Freelance is still the easiest on-ramp

Upwork and Fiverr get slandered a lot online. They also paid for my laptop. For a self-taught teenager without a CS degree, they're a cheat code.

## The gap

We ship a lot. We share methods less. There's an incredible amount of tacit knowledge in Dhaka developers' heads that never becomes a blog post or a talk. Writing this one is my small dent in that.
