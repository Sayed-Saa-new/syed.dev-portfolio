I'm still in HSC. Most of what I know about shipping software I learned by shipping software — usually badly, then a little less badly. Here's what actually worked.

## Ship small things, publicly

The first ten repos on my [GitHub](https://github.com/abushaidislam) are half-broken. That's fine. Every one of them taught me something a tutorial couldn't:

- how deploys break at 2 AM
- what a real bug report looks like
- why "it works on my machine" is a personality flaw

## Read more code than you write

For every hour I spend writing, I try to spend one reading source of libraries I use — Next.js internals, shadcn/ui components, Supabase auth. Nothing has raised my level faster.

## Pick boring stacks for real projects

I like new frameworks. I don't ship with them until they've been boring for a year. TypeScript + Next.js + Postgres is boring on purpose.

## Build in public, but don't perform

Posting progress keeps me honest. Turning every commit into a thread does not. There's a difference between accountability and marketing.
