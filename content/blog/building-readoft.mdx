I read a lot. I also forget a lot. [Readoft](https://github.com/abushaidislam/ReadOft-N) started as a personal fix for the second problem.

## The pitch

Highlight a passage in whatever you're reading. Readoft summarises it, links it to other passages you highlighted before, and quizzes you on it a week later. It's Anki plus a librarian plus a search engine over your own reading history.

## The interesting problems

- **Chunking** — books don't respect token limits. Splitting on paragraphs beats splitting on characters every time.
- **Retrieval** — vector search finds "similar wording", but readers want "similar idea". A hybrid of embeddings + keyword + tag filter gave much better results than any single approach.
- **Quiz generation** — the model happily generates trivia. Getting it to generate *useful* recall prompts required an example bank and a hard word limit.

## Stack

- Next.js + Supabase (pgvector)
- OpenAI embeddings, Claude for generation
- A tiny React Native wrapper for on-the-go highlighting

## What's next

Public reading lists, shared annotations for study groups, and offline-first sync. If you're a heavy reader and want early access — reach out.
